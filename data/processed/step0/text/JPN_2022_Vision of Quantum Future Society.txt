# 1. Introduction

## <Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy Initiatives>

In January 2020, the government formulated the Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy. To establish a hub for integrated industry-academia-government collaboration spanning fundamental research, technology demonstration, open innovation, intellectual property management, and human resource development in quantum technology, eight Quantum Innovation Hubs (QIH) were established in February 2021.Furthermore, in July of the same year, the Quantum Innovation Initiative Council (QII Council), based at the University of Tokyo, facilitated the first-ever introduction of an actual commercial quantum computer from overseas into Japan.
In the industrial sector, the Quantum Technology-based New Industry Creation Council (Q-STAR) was established in September of the same year, advancing the development of a new framework to systematically promote the research, development, and industrialization of quantum technologies.
Furthermore, concurrent with the formulation of this strategy, a forward-looking technology roadmap was also developed. Based on this roadmap, research and development is steadily progressing in various fields, including quantum computing and quantum simulation, quantum measurement and sensing, and quantum communication and cryptography.
Furthermore, international joint research between the Quantum Innovation Hub (QIH) and overseas research centers in Europe, the United States, and elsewhere is progressing steadily. This includes the hosting of the "International Symposium on Quantum Science and Technology Innovation
(Quantum Innovation 2021).

## <Environmental Changes Since Formulating the Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy>

Based on the formulation of the Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy in January 2020, Japan has steadily advanced its research and development efforts. However, since the strategy's formulation, the environment surrounding quantum technology has undergone rapid and significant changes.
Overseas, private companies are leading the charge, accelerating efforts to research, develop, and commercialize quantum computers with ambitious goals.Furthermore, technologies and services integrating quantum computing with conventional (classical) computing systems are also developing rapidly. Amidst this intensifying international competition, Japan is expected to fundamentally accelerate and strengthen its quantum computer R&D, while industry, academia, and government work together to undertake full-scale, strategic efforts aimed at enhancing industrial competitiveness.
Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the digital transformation (DX) of the entire socio-economic system. Moving forward, efforts toward Society 5.0—a vision of a highly integrated cyber-physical space—are expected to advance further. Consequently, as data volumes and communication traffic explode, and security demands intensify, the importance of quantum technology—with its superior computational power and confidentiality—is growing significantly.
Furthermore, in response to the worsening climate change crisis, efforts to achieve a carbon-neutral society are gaining full momentum. Quantum technology is expected to significantly contribute to realizing this carbon-neutral society by enabling dramatic improvements in future computing performance and facilitating decarbonization through the development of unprecedented environmental materials.
Achieving large-scale quantum computers and their practical application faces numerous technical challenges, such as generating stable qubits, requiring various breakthrough technologies. In recent years, innovations in computer science, including AI , and cutting-edge research in quantum information science have made significant progress.
Remarkable progress has been made in foundational technologies for controlling and elucidating these physical phenomena, including the development of sophisticated quantum state control techniques utilizing laser technology. It is crucial to advance strategic research and development toward future breakthrough technologies while mobilizing knowledge from numerous academic fields and vigorously promoting broad-based fundamental research and human resource development, including young researchers.
In recent years, the importance of economic security has grown significantly, driven not only by the global semiconductor shortage triggered by the COVID-19 pandemic but also by the emergence of geopolitical challenges.Quantum technology is also critically important for economic security. It is vital for nations to possess advanced quantum technology domestically, including acquiring cutting-edge technologies to secure technical superiority and ensuring supply chains for critical components and materials. Furthermore, stable and continuous human resource development for this purpose is essential. Additionally, safeguarding important data and information systems that form the foundation of industry and society, including technical information, from cyberattacks and other threats is crucial.

## Responding to Environmental Changes

Since the formulation of the Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy in January 2020, expectations for the role of quantum technology in addressing rapidly changing socioeconomic environments have grown. This includes intensifying international competition in quantum computing, the rapid advancement of DX driven by the COVID-19 pandemic, and efforts toward realizing a carbon-neutral society.
Furthermore, as the importance of economic security continues to grow, quantum technology is also critically important for economic security. It is vital to possess advanced technologies domestically and cultivate human resources.
Therefore, accelerating and strengthening efforts in quantum technology R&D, societal implementation, industrialization, and other areas more than ever before is an urgent task. This will ensure international competitiveness while creating growth opportunities for Japan's industries and addressing societal challenges.
The rapid advancement and widespread adoption of AI are driving the sophistication and expansion of data utilization. Quantum technology will serve as a catalyst to further accelerate this trend, bringing about a dramatic leap in future computing performance. Furthermore, quantum technology is expected to deliver significant improvements in sensing and communication capabilities, enabling vastly more sensitive sensing and highly secure communications compared to conventional methods.
Quantum technology, which will achieve such dramatic improvements in future computing, sensing, and communication capabilities, holds promise across diverse fields including drug discovery and healthcare, materials, finance, energy, lifestyle services, transportation, logistics, manufacturing, and safety and security.
Quantum technology can only create value for the socio-economic system when applied across various fields. Therefore, when promoting research and development, social implementation, industrialization, and other initiatives related to quantum technology, it is crucial to adopt a comprehensive perspective that integrates quantum technology into the entire socio-economic system.
Furthermore, quantum technology is closely intertwined with conventional (classical) technology systems in fields such as computer science (including AI and advanced simulation), information and communication technologies (including 5G/Beyond 5G), semiconductors, and measurement/sensing technologies. It is vital to advance initiatives by integrating and unifying quantum technology with these systems.
Against this backdrop, this vision is formulated as a new strategy for the future society, aiming to realize a transformation of society as a whole. It will be developed in coordination with strategies such as the AI Strategy, the Beyond 5G Promotion Strategy, and the Semiconductor Strategy, while also considering collaboration with like-minded nations. Industry, academia, and government will work together to utilize quantum technology to create growth opportunities for Japan's industries, such as a productivity revolution, and to solve societal challenges like achieving carbon neutrality.

## <Relationship between this Vision and the Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy>

This vision is formulated as a "strategy for societal transformation through quantum technology"—a future societal vision and the strategy for achieving it—aimed at leveraging quantum technology to create growth opportunities for Japan's industries, solve societal challenges, and realize transformation across society as a whole. This contrasts with the "Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy" (essentially a "quantum technology R&D strategy"), which focuses primarily on quantum technology R&D and related fields.Therefore, the government will advance its initiatives through the dual pillars of this Vision (Strategy for Social Transformation through Quantum Technology) and the "Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy" (Strategy for Quantum Technology R&D).
Therefore, moving forward, we will continue to advance research and development efforts based on the "Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy" formulated in January 2020 (with partial revisions to the roadmap). Furthermore, guided by this vision, we will promote initiatives to utilize quantum technology for creating growth opportunities for Japan's industries—such as a productivity revolution—and solving societal challenges like achieving carbon neutrality. We will advance efforts to realize the transformation of society as a whole, with an eye toward the future.
This vision was formulated with an eye toward the future society envisioned through quantum technology and the desired state to be achieved by 2030. However, as the environment surrounding quantum technology is expected to continue changing rapidly, we will continuously adapt to future environmental changes, including reviewing this vision.

## (3) Contributing to Carbon Neutrality and SDGs

In response to the worsening climate change crisis, countries worldwide are accelerating environmental initiatives such as carbon neutrality and beyond zero (negative emissions). Japan aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 46% by fiscal year 2030 (compared to fiscal year 2013 levels). Achieving this goal requires fundamental transformation of socioeconomic systems toward decarbonization across sectors including industry, residential, and transportation.
Furthermore, to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs: Sustainable Development Goals), faces numerous complex social challenges requiring resolution, such as healthcare, food security, and poverty. Additionally, Japan itself faces social challenges that must be overcome to build a sustainable society, including an aging population with declining birth rates and the resulting decrease in the working-age population.
Quantum technology is expected to significantly contribute to achieving a carbon-neutral society and solving complex social challenges such as the SDGs. It will enable dramatic improvements in future computing and sensing capabilities, facilitate decarbonization through the development of unprecedented environmental materials, realize a healthy and long-lived society through early disease diagnosis and treatment, and enhance productivity amid a shrinking working-age population.

## (4) Advancements in Foundational Technologies Supporting Quantum Computers

Achieving large-scale quantum computers and their practical application requires numerous technological breakthroughs, including stable qubit generation and error correction. To advance research and development of these breakthrough technologies, strategic initiatives must be complemented by broad-based fundamental research grounded in theoretical approaches, which will pave the way for future breakthroughs.For instance, to generate and utilize stable qubits, it is necessary to deepen fundamental understanding of dissipative phenomena and many-body interactions within quantum systems operating in non-equilibrium states or open systems, such as those experiencing decoherence.In physics, understanding these phenomena while accounting for external environmental influences has generally been a difficult problem. However, in recent years, foundational technologies for controlling and elucidating these physical phenomena have seen remarkable development. This includes innovations in computer science, including AI; advances in quantum simulation experiments using cooled atomic systems; and the pioneering of sophisticated control technologies for quantum states utilizing cutting-edge laser technology.
It is crucial to advance strategic research and development toward breakthrough technologies essential for the future scaling and practical application of quantum computers, while also mobilizing knowledge from numerous academic fields and vigorously promoting broad-based fundamental research and human resource development, including young researchers.

## (5) Economic Security of Quantum Technologies

In recent years, the importance of economic security has grown significantly, driven not only by the global semiconductor shortage triggered by the COVID-19 pandemic but also by the emergence of geopolitical challenges. Quantum technology is poised to play a foundational role in supporting future socioeconomic systems, making it critically important for economic security. It can be considered a pivotal technology at the core of future competition for global supremacy among nations.
Therefore, from an economic security perspective, it is necessary to possess advanced quantum technologies domestically, including acquiring cutting-edge technologies to secure technical superiority in quantum technology and ensuring supply chains for critical foundational components and materials. This requires the continuous and stable development and retention of human resources for this purpose. Furthermore, it is important to take measures to protect critical information systems and data that form the foundation of industry and society, including technical information, from cyberattacks and other threats.

# 6. Finally

The 20th century, which saw the birth of quantum technology, witnessed the successive emergence of new technologies based on quantum mechanics—such as nuclear power, semiconductor electronics originating from the transistor, and lasers— which drove scientific and technological innovation and societal progress.
However, quantum mechanics encompasses principles unique to itself, such as the collapse of a wave function—where a superposition state collapses into a single value upon observation—and quantum entanglement. These represent a qualitative departure from the classical physics we are familiar with and became subjects for academic inquiry, such as the measurement problem in quantum theory.By the latter half of the 20th century, it came to be positioned as a dream technology capable of bringing about new technological innovations.
Since the start of this century, advances in both experimental and theoretical research have propelled quantum technology from "verification" to "control" at a rapid pace. For instance, in quantum computing, the once-distant dream of precisely controlling the quantum states of individual atoms and electrons for use in quantum computation is becoming a reality.
In the future, it will become possible to control larger-scale quantum systems. This will usher in an era where quantum technology is fully utilized for information processing, secure communications, and ultra-high-sensitivity sensing. This vision outlines the fundamental concepts and direction of efforts for effectively utilizing quantum technology in this new era.
These are technologies humanity has yet to master, and they are expected to transform the future socio-economic landscape and bring unprecedented benefits. Conversely, however, if used with malicious intent, they could cause immense harm. Therefore, we must proactively engage with these new technologies, grasping their very essence, and ensure they are harnessed as humanity's shared assets. This means not only pursuing economic growth but also striving for harmony between people and the environment, and aiming for human well-being—a life enriched by the heart. We should lead the innovation in this field.
The advancement of quantum technology to date is the result of researchers' curiosity and diligent efforts. Going forward, industry will play a major role in delivering the benefits of quantum technology to society.
We look forward to industry and academia joining hands to further advance quantum technology and bring significant benefits to the socio-economic sphere.
