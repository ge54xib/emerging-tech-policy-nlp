# I. Introduction

- ✓ We are approaching the 100th anniversary of 1925, the year quantum mechanics is said to have been born.Japan has long held strengths in fundamental quantum theory. In its 2016 Fifth Science and Technology Basic Plan, the government positioned quantum technology as a "foundational technology possessing strengths that form the core of new value creation," and formulated the "Quantum Technology Innovation Strategy" in 2020.
- ✓ Meanwhile, overseas, the wave of industrialization for broad quantum information technologies arrived early. Industry-academia-government consortia were launched in the United States in 2018 and in Europe in 2021, showing significant expansion of investment from industry.Against this backdrop, Japan also launched the "Quantum Technology-based New Industry Creation Council (Q-STA R )"1(<>)on September 1, 2021. The government subsequently formulated the "Quantum Future Society Vision" in April 2022 and the "Quantum Future Industry Creation Strategy" in April 2023, advancing initiatives toward their realization with concerted efforts from industry, academia, and government.
- ✓ Nevertheless, progress in quantum technology—a cutting-edge field—remains remarkable worldwide, with integration across diverse sectors advancing.Therefore, considering the changes in the environment surrounding Japan since the formulation of these government strategies, it is expected that strategic efforts will be made, including not only the industrialization of quantum technology itself but also strengthening countermeasures to enhance the productivity of industries driven by quantum technology and to create and develop new industries originating in Japan (hereafter referred to as quantum industries).

# II. Positioning of This Policy

This policy compiles measures identified by the Quantum Technology Innovation Council as necessary to promptly strengthen, concretize, or supplement existing strategies. It complements the three existing quantum technology strategies 2(<>)to ensure Japan's quantum technology can compete globally, addressing current challenges and outlining required actions.

### (Quantum Security / Quantum Networks)

- ✓ Concerns have been raised that the widespread public-key cryptography algorithms currently used on the internet (such as RSA cryptography and elliptic curve cryptography) could be compromised by the realization of quantum computers. Consequently, there is a demand for cryptographic technologies that remain secure in the quantum computing era.The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST; USA) is advancing international standardization efforts for post-quantum cryptography (PQC), with the first international standards expected to be finalized in 2025.
- ✓ Quantum cryptography is an encryption system that can reliably detect eavesdropping due to the physical properties of quantum mechanics. Its adoption is anticipated in fields handling sensitive information, particularly among government and financial users.
- ✓ Japanese companies possess world-leading technology in quantum key distribution (QKD) devices, which utilize quantum properties for cryptographic key delivery, excelling in aspects such as key generation speed and transmission distance. Commercialization has been achieved. However, other nations are also actively pursuing research and development related to quantum cryptography. They are constructing test beds combining ground-based and satellite systems and conducting proof-of-concept experiments aimed at commercialization, intensifying international competition.Whether Japan can continue to be a world leader in quantum cryptography over the next few years remains to be seen.
- ✓ Regarding quantum relay technology, envisioned for use in quantum cryptography and quantum internet, international development competition is intensifying. This includes demonstrations of quantum entanglement relay and long-distance entanglement delivery using quantum memory. Quantum relay technology is essential for advancing quantum technology, as it can connect quantum computers. Therefore, Japan must elevate its quantum relay technology to the world's top level.
- ✓ The Quantum Security Center (National Institute of Information and Communications Technology,hereinafter "NICT") plans to thoroughly utilize the quantum cryptography testbed, whose expansion and enhancement will be completed in March 2024, in collaboration with government and financial sector users. This will advance efforts to clarify challenges for social implementation and promote adoption by early adopters.

# IV. New Perspectives for Creating and Developing the Quantum Industry

During the so-called "dawn period" of quantum technology, industrialization progressed primarily through fundamental and applied research driven by researchers' creative ideas. Now, as the utilization of quantum technology begins, the key to further development lies not only in the parallel advancement of fundamental and applied research but also in international collaboration with like-minded nations from a global perspective.
Therefore, when considering and promoting measures for the creation and development of the quantum industry, based on the "Three Perspectives for Advancing Practical Application and Industrialization" outlined in the Quantum Future Industry Creation Strategy Three Perspectives 7(<>)for Advancing Practical Application and Industrialization" outlined in the Quantum Future Industry Creation Strategy. To further strengthen initiatives related to global collaboration and enhancement, a new perspective, "Globalization," is established and added. Going forward, efforts will be based on these four perspectives.
■ Globalization: "Enhancing Global Visibility through Strategic Collaboration with Like-Minded Nations and Japan's Original Value Creation"
A gainst the backdrop of rapid advancements in technological trends and industrialization, nations are increasingly aiming to capture the global quantum technology market. For Japan to compete in this global market, it is crucial to strategically collaborate with like-minded nations. First, we must prioritize the overseas expansion of competitive products and services through concerted public-private efforts. Following this, we will advance branding efforts to establish Japan as the global leader in quantum technology ("When it comes to quantum, think Japan"), and subsequently pursue the continuous securing of market share and the construction of an ecosystem.
Furthermore, the practical application of quantum technology faces challenges that cannot necessarily be addressed by extending current technologies, such as scaling quantum computers beyond 1,000 qubits. Solving these challenges requires breakthroughs based on fundamental principles.Therefore, precisely in these high-barrier fields, it is vital to return to fundamentals (Back to the Basics). Beyond ongoing efforts to build R&D and testbed environments aimed at practical application and industrialization, we must unite industry, academia, and government to develop next-generation technologies and cultivate the talent to create them, thereby enhancing global visibility (Visibility).

# V. Key challenges and response directions based on new perspectives

## (1) Strengthening Expansion into Global Markets

### (Challenges)

- ✓ While global collaboration, such as hosting international conferences, is steadily progressing through the utilization of MOUs between Q-STAR and overseas industry associations, and between AIST and overseas research institutions, there are voices stating that Japan still lacks the necessary local information and networks required for the overseas expansion of services utilizing quantum technology.
- ✓ Furthermore, opinions exist that overseas expansion by private companies alone carries high risk precisely because the quantum technology market is emerging. Additionally, use cases explaining the advantages of quantum technology from a cost-effectiveness perspective remain insufficient to expand investment from both domestic and international sources.
- ✓ Additionally, it has been pointed out that the scale of domestic venture capital (VC) firms capable of evaluating deep tech like quantum technology and making long-term investments remains insufficient. Furthermore, opportunities for quantum startups/ventures to establish themselves and fully expand into domestic and international markets—essential for forming a quantum ecosystem—are also deemed inadequate.

### (Response Direction)

- ✓ The government will take the lead in creating and securing opportunities for global expansion by leveraging frameworks for intergovernmental collaboration, promoting smooth communication with overseas stakeholders including industry, universities, and research institutions. These efforts will enhance the security of global supply chains centered on like-minded nations, enabling Japan to secure a strategically indispensable position.
- ✓ Strengthen the development of testbeds designed with diverse domestic and international users in mind to enrich opportunities for quantum technology utilization and build a portfolio of use cases sought by companies and VCs.
- ✓ We will promote the construction of a quantum ecosystem by providing information on overseas markets to quantum startups/venture companies, further facilitating opportunities for them to connect with VCs and domestic companies possessing overseas networks, supporting business development utilizing national research institute facilities and equipment, and promoting the use of quantum technology, primarily by national and public institutions as early adopters.

## (2) Strongly Promoting Japan's Globally Recognized Technology Development

### (Challenge)

- ✓ Due in part to the impact of COVID-19, Japan's participation in international conferences and number of international joint research projects have been relatively low in recent years, risking reduced visibility within the global quantum community, particularly in Europe and the US.
- ✓ When advancing international collaboration with like-minded nations, it is crucial to accurately assess overseas trends while pursuing win-win relationships. Furthermore, since technology can eventually be caught up with through overwhelming scale, Japan must continuously secure internationally significant quantum technologies domestically to maintain its advantage.
- ✓ Quantum technology is closely linked to economic security. Even equipment currently available from overseas may become inaccessible to Japan as technology advances and industrialization progresses.

### (Response Direction)

- ✓ The government will take the lead in creating and securing opportunities for global expansion by leveraging frameworks for intergovernmental collaboration, promoting smooth communication with overseas stakeholders including industry, universities, and research institutions.
- ✓ Academia should continuously promote fundamental and foundational research with a medium-to-longterm perspective to avoid being consumed solely by technological competition focused on catching up with global research trends. Furthermore, it should strategically leverage these outcomes to secure intellectual property rights while steadily advancing international standardization activities.
- ✓ Flexibly utilize advanced devices and peripheral equipment, such as commercially leading overseas quantum computers ( ), to create use cases and accumulate know-how. For technologies critical to the global supply chain, Japan will independently advance research and development (R&D) and promote domestic production ( ) without excessive reliance on other countries ( ).

## (3) Cultivating Quantum Talent in Industry and Academia

### (Challenge)

- ✓ As markets for quantum technology utilization emerge globally, there is a recognized shortage of global talent, particularly those capable of deploying quantum-enabled services and building future supply chains. Developing such talent takes time, necessitating immediate action.
- ✓ For Japan to maintain its presence within the world's top research communities over the medium to long term, it is also necessary to enhance visibility. This can be achieved by enabling researchers to gain experience within these top global research communities at an early stage, thereby securing opportunities for future participation in international conferences and similar forums.

# (Direction of Response)

- ✓ Universities, research institutions, and industry must collaborate to enrich opportunities for young people to encounter and learn about quantum technology, from elementary and junior high school through technical colleges and universities.
- ✓ Expand opportunities for doctoral students and young researchers to gain early exposure to the world's top research communities through increased overseas assignments and by creating an environment that attracts international talent. Additionally, expand contributions to top research community activities, such as having Japanese researchers serve as chairs at prominent international conferences.

## (2) Initiatives to Promote Japan's Technological Development

○ Quantum Computers (Software and Hardware)

### <Establishing User-Friendly Test Beds>

- ✓ Since few companies have experience using actual quantum computers, it is necessary to accelerate the development of testbeds from a user perspective and ensure their stable maintenance and management.
- ✓ Therefore, at RIKEN, using a domestically developed actual machine (a superconducting quantum computer) as a test bed, we will develop and advance foundational technologies from software to hardware, as well as system software for quantum-classical hybrid computing environments. We will also conduct fundamental research and development involving industry and academia, including next-generation technologies, to further promote its utilization.
- ✓ Furthermore, AIST will advance the construction of a hybrid quantum-classical computing environment testbed (including quantum-inspired technologies) aimed at creating industrial use cases. This will involve introducing large-scale GPU computers and quantum computers, utilizing these actual machines, quantum simulators, and various cloud services (both domestic and international). AIST will also establish an accessible framework for use, including by overseas institutions, thereby strengthening its hub function in international collaboration.
- ✓ In doing so, rather than having each company or user build a distributed computing environment for each use case, we will maximize the use of the established testbed and hub functions. While developing user manuals and accumulating know-how, we will aim for the early creation of use cases suitable for industrialization across various fields.

### <Initiatives with an Eye Toward Building a Global Supply Chain>

- ✓ AIST will increase opportunities for initial verification of technological advantages and business viability through quantum technology, with an eye toward building a global supply chain.
- ✓ To this end, based on the latest trends in quantum computer development, we will focus on scaling up and integrating components essential for quantum computer industrialization—such as quantum chips and cryogenic control circuits—while anticipating collaboration with quantum computer vendors across various architectures. To achieve this, AIST will establish evaluation testbeds for next-generation materials and electronic devices, including prototype equipment and cryogenic environments, while enhancing existing facilities.
- ✓
Through these initiatives, we will incorporate new technologies and equipment based on advancements in quantum technology among domestic and international quantum computer vendors and component manufacturers.
We will foster an environment that supports corporate activities such as supplier development and international standardization efforts.

### ＜Identifying Killer Use Cases＞

- ✓ To advance the utilization of quantum computers, it is crucial to demonstrate to corporate executives and investors use cases where quantum computers play a decisive role—such as challenges difficult to address with current classical computing power or those with high societal value.

- "killer use cases") that quantum computers can decisively address, and explore connecting this to the development of killer applications. In doing so, it is important to accumulate know-how while using the highest-performance computational environments possible, enabling rapid adaptation when practical quantum computers are developed.

### <Addressing the Shift to Edge Computing>

- ✓ With the advent of the IoT era, the volume of data circulating globally is exploding. Projections indicate it will reach approximately 180 times the 2010 level (about 180 zettabytes) by 2025. Conventional computers are nearing their limits in handling this data processing, particularly regarding the advancement of semiconductor microfabrication technology and the resulting increase in power consumption.
- ✓ Against this backdrop of an anticipated shift towards data-centric edge computing, there is a growing need for the development of software capable of performing more effective and efficient computations using quantum computers, as well as middleware that allows users to leverage this capability seamlessly without conscious effort.
✓ Therefore, Osaka University will continue to systematically conduct research and development spanning software, middleware, and hardware. This includes building a cloud environment for integrated control of quantum and classical computers, developing general-purpose quantum middleware to realize platforms where usage is defined by quantum software, and conducting fundamental and applied research in quantum AI.

We will also pursue the exploration of new technologies that fuse such quantum technologies with the biofield.

### <Strengthening R&D and Supply Infrastructure for Quantum Materials>

- ✓ Quantum materials can exhibit outstanding functionality through the control of quantum states of electrons and spins. They are indispensable for developing various devices and components, such as quantum computers, quantum sensing, and quantum cryptography communications. Japanese companies currently hold significant market shares in some areas.
- ✓ Furthermore, in the development of components for next-generation devices, there is also expectation for the establishment of stable manufacturing technologies for quantum materials and the construction of supply systems, alongside the exploration of semiconductor materials with novel physical properties that are difficult to achieve with silicon.
- ✓ Therefore, we will advance the enhancement of quantum device performance and the strengthening of supply systems, including the improvement of quality and the establishment of large crystal fabrication technologies for quantum materials such as diamond, which is highly anticipated. Simultaneously, we will promote the development of quantum materials with novel properties, such as topological materials and two-dimensional materials.

- ✓ Furthermore, through public-private collaboration, we will support: promoting participation in service demonstrations premised on overseas expansion; building global supply chains centered on like-minded nations; fostering collaboration and M&A with local companies and global players; marketing packages combining quantum technology with technologies and products where Japan already leads globally; and organizing exhibitions at events hosted by countries and local governments expected to attract significant international participation.

### <Support for Startups/Venture Companies Utilizing National Research Institutions>

- ✓ Given the uncertainty surrounding future quantum technologies, it is extremely challenging for startups/ventures with limited capital to independently procure, maintain, and manage all the massive equipment required for developing and evaluating superconducting quantum computer hardware and components, such as dilution refrigerators.
- ✓ Consequently, the number of startups/ventures developing hardware like quantum computers and quantum devices in Japan is significantly limited compared to software development. Conversely, overseas, it is not uncommon for startups/ventures to lead quantum computer development based on technologies developed in academia.
- ✓ Therefore, to promote the establishment of quantum startups/ventures and new ventures, considering the current status of national research institutes 8 (<>), we will explore establishing systems enabling businesses to utilize facilities and equipment owned by national research institutes for product development, production, and service provision, as well as expanding the range of available facilities.
- ✓ Furthermore, since the number of startups/ventures originating from national research institutes in Japan is even more limited than those originating from universities, we will also examine mechanisms to support the creation of startups/ventures utilizing national research institute research outcomes and technology transfer. This includes, for example, providing incentives to researchers at national research institutes who are not permitted to engage in paid side jobs, based on the current status of systems at each national research institute 9 (<>).

### <Utilizing Quantum Technologies as an Early Adopter>

- ✓ To create new markets through promising technologies and research outcomes from quantum startups/ventures, it is crucial for the government and other entities not only to provide funding but also to become the first buyers (early adopters) of these startups/ventures.
- ✓ In Japan, commercialization of quantum computers and quantum cryptography is coming into view, making this a critical time to accelerate industrialization and ecosystem development efforts.
- ✓ Therefore, when commercial quantum computers developed by domestic companies are released, public institutions and local governments will procure them promptly and encourage their adoption by user companies, thereby supporting the stimulation of initial demand and business expansion.
- ✓ Regarding quantum cryptography communications, NICT will advance the clarification of challenges and establishment of use cases for societal implementation through the utilization of its testbed. It will also promote adoption consideration by government users, who are anticipated to be early adopters.

# VII. Conclusion

This strategy, compiled by the Quantum Technology Innovation Council, outlines the additional measures Japan must take to strengthen its position and ensure the creation and development of a quantum industry within the rapidly advancing and intensifying global competition in quantum technology.
It focuses particularly on expanding international collaboration, supporting corporate overseas expansion, and further strengthening Japan's globally recognized technological development capabilities— essential for these goals—along with nurturing the human resources to support them.
Regarding international collaboration, Japan will advance the cultivation and securing of its own technologies while utilizing various frameworks with like-minded nations. It is necessary to strengthen foundational research for next-generation quantum technologies and promote industry participation by clarifying technical specifications and establishing roadmaps essential for scaling up and industrializing components, materials, and devices critical for quantum technology practical application.
Regarding initiatives to advance Japan's technological development, in the quantum computing field, efforts include: establishing user-friendly testbeds such as hybrid computing environments designed for creating industrial use cases; developing so-called "killer applications"; actively participating in international standardization activities; and supporting the identification of practical challenges, industrialization, and overseas market expansion for quantum-inspired technologies. Flexible revisions to national project goals and portfolios based on global development trends are also necessary. technologies, supporting industrialization and expansion into overseas markets, and flexibly reviewing national project goals and portfolios based on global development trends. In the quantum security and network field
In the quantum security and networking field, it is necessary to conduct demonstration tests for the early societal implementation of quantum cryptographic communications, aiming for societal implementation by fiscal year 2030. Additionally, it is essential to promote research and development of quantum relay technologies, which are indispensable for realizing quantum internet and other applications, advance international standardization, and promote the compilation of evaluation criteria and methods required for establishing international certification systems for QKD devices.In the quantum measurement/sensing and materials field, efforts are needed to build testbeds simulating real-world environments, establish user access points, and improve the quality of materials like diamonds, while also advancing the development of novel quantum materials with unique properties.
Regarding human resource development, it is necessary to advance broad-based training not only for university students but also for technical college students, working professionals, and others. This includes cultivating researchers, engineers, managers, evangelists, and personnel capable of overseeing the entire system integration required for the industrialization of quantum technology. This should be done in collaboration with relevant organizations such as quantum technology innovation hubs. Furthermore, accelerating personnel exchanges and recruitment with like-minded countries is essential.
Regarding strengthening the innovation foundation, it is necessary to support the creation and scaling of startups/venture companies by promoting participation in service demonstrations premised on overseas expansion through public-private collaboration, and by building global supply chains centered on like-minded countries.
Furthermore, it is essential to coordinate quantum technology innovation measures with strategies for other cutting-edge critical technologies—such as AI, semiconductors, biotechnology, materials, and information and communications—to advance the development and securing of technologies indispensable to Japan and foster new industries.
